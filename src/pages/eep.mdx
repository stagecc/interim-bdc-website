<PageContent title="BDC External Expert Panel">

The BDC External Expert Panel (EEP) is an independent body of experts that informs and advises NHLBI Leadership about the development of the ecosystem. Members of the EEP volunteer their time and insights over a multi-year period. The individuals listed below are current members of the BDC EEP.

<EepMember imageName="bader-david">
### David A. Bader, Ph.D.
**Distinguished Professor<br/>
New Jersey Institute of Technology**

David A. Bader is a Distinguished Professor and founder of the Department of Data Science in the Ying Wu College of Computing and Director of the Institute for Data Science at New Jersey Institute of Technology. Prior to this, he served as founding Professor and Chair of the School of Computational Science and Engineering, College of Computing, at Georgia Institute of Technology. Bader is an elected Board Member of the Computing Research Association (CRA). He is a Fellow of the IEEE, ACM, AAAS, and SIAM; a recipient of the IEEE Sidney Fernbach Award; and the 2022 Innovation Hall of Fame inductee of the University of Maryland's A. James Clark School of Engineering. The Computer History Museum recognizes Bader for developing the first Linux-based supercomputer, which became the predominant architecture for all major supercomputers in the world.
</EepMember>

<EepMember imageName="craven-mark">
### Mark Craven, Ph.D.
**Professor<br/>
Department of Biostatistics & Medical Informatics<br/>
University of Wisconsin**

Mark Craven is a professor in the Department of Biostatistics and Medical Informatics at the University of Wisconsin and an affiliate faculty member in the Department of Computer Sciences. The focus of his research program is to develop and apply machine-learning methods to infer models of networks of interactions among genes, proteins, clinical variables, environmental factors, and phenotypes of interest. He is the Co-Director of the NIH/NLM-funded Computation and Informatics in Biology and Medicine (CIBM) Training Program and a member of the Center for Genomic Science Innovation, the Institute for Clinical and Translational Research, and the Carbone Cancer Center at the University of Wisconsin.  He also serves on the Genomic Data Science Working Group for the National Human Genome Research Institute (NHGRI) and the Safety Monitoring Board for the Lower Urinary Tract Dysfunction Research Network (LURN) study, which is sponsored by the National Institute of Diabetes and Digestive and Kidney Diseases (NIDDK).
</EepMember>

<EepMember imageName="mendelson-david">
### David S. Mendelson, M.D.
**Professor<br/>
Diagnostic, Molecular, and Interventional Radiology<br/>
Windreich Department of Artificial Intelligence and Human Health<br/>
Icahn School of Medicine at Mount Sinai**

David Mendelson is a Professor of Radiology at the Icahn School of Medicine, Vice Chair of Radiology IT for the Mount Sinai Health System, and Associate CMIO of the Mount Sinai Doctor Faculty Practice. He has been deeply involved in the evolution of Healthcare Informatics since 1992. He has led the implementation, integration, and maintenance of numerous large-scale systems while actively conducting large clinical imaging research projects.  Dr. Mendelson served on the Radiological Society of North America Radiology Informatics Committee and is Co-Chair of the Integrating the Healthcare Enterprise International Board of Directors. He was the PI on the NIBIB contract to enable Image sharing over the internet, which has evolved into a formal program with Carequality to facilitate national standards-based image exchange.  In his current role at Mount Sinai a major focus is the introduction of AI technologies both for Radiology and the broader healthcare community. 
</EepMember>

<EepMember imageName="parashar-manish">
### Manish Parashar, Ph.D.
**Director, Scientific Computing and Imaging (SCI) Institute<br/>
Presidential Professor, Kahlert School of Computing <br/>
University of Utah**

Manish Parashar is Director of the Scientific Computing and Imaging (SCI) Institute, Chair in Computational Science and Engineering, and Presidential Professor, Kalhert School of Computing at the University of Utah. He is currently leading the One-Utah Responsible AI Initiative aimed at responsibly leveraging AI to address urgent regional challenges. He recently completed an IPA appointment at the National Science Foundation as Office Director of the NSF Office of Advanced Cyberinfrastructure, where he oversaw investments in national cyberinfrastructure. He also served as co-chair of the National Science and Technology Council's Subcommittee on the Future Advanced Ecosystem and the National Artificial Intelligence Research Resource Task Force. Parashar's expertise is in high performance parallel and distributed computing and cyberinfrastructure. Parashar is the founding chair of the IEEE Technical Consortium on High Performance Computing (TCHPC) and is a Fellow of AAAS, ACM, and IEEE. 
</EepMember>

<EepMember imageName="ritchie-marylyn">
### Marylyn D. Ritchie, Ph.D.
**Edward Rose, M.D. and Elizabeth Kirk Rose, M.D. Professor of Genetics<br/>
Director of the Institute for Biomedical Informatics<br/>
University of Pennsylvania School of Medicine**

Marylyn D. Ritchie is the Director of the Institute for Biomedical Informatics, the Director of the Division of Informatics, Department of Biostatistics, Epidemiology, and Informatics, and the Vice Dean of Artificial Intelligence and Computing for the Perelman School of Medicine at the University of Pennsylvania. She is also the Vice President for Research Informatics at the University of Pennsylvania Health System. Dr. Ritchie is an expert in computational genomics, bioinformatics, epistasis, pharmacogenomics, big data, evolutionary computation, genetic epidemiology, statistical genetics, systems genomics, computational biology, translational informatics, and cardiovascular disease. Dr. Ritchie received her Ph.D. in Statistical Genetics and M.S. in Applied Statistics from Vanderbilt University and her B.S. in Biology from the University of Pittsburgh at Johnstown.
</EepMember>

<EepMember imageName="washington-talitha">
### Talitha Washington, Ph.D.
**Professor of Mathematics<br/>
Clark Atlanta University**

Talitha Washington is a tenured professor of mathematics at Clark Atlanta University and an affiliate faculty at Morehouse College, Morehouse School of Medicine, and Spelman College. Dr. Washington is the Director of the Atlanta University Center (AUC) Data Science Initiative and the Lead Principal Investigator of the National Science Foundation (NSF) National Data Science Alliance. She is the President of the Association for Women in Mathematics (AWM) and was, most recently, a Program Director at the NSF. Her research interests include applied mathematics, dynamical systems, nonstandard finite difference schemes, data science, artificial intelligence, and education. Dr. Washington completed her undergraduate studies in mathematics at Spelman College and her master's and doctoral degrees in mathematics from the University of Connecticut. She is a Fellow of the AWM, the American Mathematical Society, and the American Association for the Advancement of Science.
</EepMember>
